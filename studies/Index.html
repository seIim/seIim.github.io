AST499 General Relativity

In the Fall22 semester I worked with Dr. Shashi Kanbur in the Physics Department to complete an independent study in General Relativity. The book used was A First Course in General Relativity, by Bernard Shutz . To complete this independent study, I gave a presentation to the math and physics department where I derived the curvature tensor. That presentation is located here: General Relativity and Curvature
Later, as a project for my astrophysics class I revisited the book and worked through the derivation for the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff Equation, and presented it at QUEST, where I won a presenters award: The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff Equation

MAT499 Machine Learning

Over the Spring23 Semester Dr. John Meyers and I read through Patterns, Predictions, and Actions; a book that details an introduction to the mathematical framework of prediction and machine learning. This book starts with Bayesian prediction and works its way through supervised learning. This class culminated in projects where I would code prediction frameworks and models from scratch, using resources from the available literature.

MAT499 Geometry, Topology, and Physics

Also over the Spring23 semester Dr. Indu-Rasika and I read through the book Geometry, Topology, and Physics and did focused studies on chapters 2-7 with an emphasis on examples and problems. The independent study culminated in me presenting an interesting example from Simplicial Homology in a QUEST talk.

Differential Topology Seminar

A once-weekly seminar on topics from differential topology presented to a group of interested students by Dr. John Meyers. This seminar took place in the Spring22 semester and was what introduced me in part to differential topology, leading to my independent studies in General Relativity and Geometry and topology in Physics. The subject matter covered in this seminar highlights what I find most interesting in math, and is a subject that I hope to continue learning in the future.
